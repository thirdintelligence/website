# AI Roadmap

## Purpose

The AI Roadmap should make Third i the client's clearest, most practical partner for improving AI adoption. The goal is to identify and help implement at least one high-value service approximately two months into a suitable engagement, without turning the page into an upsell catalog.

## Page structure

### 1. Executive summary

- Client's confirmed current AI state.
- Highest-confidence near-term improvement.
- Top risks or dependencies.
- Third i's current recommendation.
- Research as-of date.

### 2. Capability matrix

Recommended top-level categories:

- Daily productivity and knowledge work
- Film and creative production
- Website design and development
- Marketing and acquisition
- Business intelligence and strategy
- Service and operations
- Communications
- Analytics and reporting
- Compliance and risk support
- Vibe-coding and AI-assisted development
- Workflow automation and agents

Split categories further when the label would hide materially different workflows.

### 3. Capability record

Each capability includes:

- outcome;
- confirmed current status;
- status label and icon;
- recommended workflow;
- tool/model class;
- value band;
- effort band;
- evidence quality;
- client prerequisites;
- privacy/compliance considerations;
- Third i recommendation;
- next practical experiment;
- research source and as-of date.

When Third i offers the workflow as a service, the record may also show confirmed deliverables, engagement shape, and an approved client-specific investment range. If pricing is not confirmed, it says `Scope required`; the page never calculates a fee from generic hours or public tool pricing.

Value, effort, evidence, and recommendation are woven into the record. There is no separate opportunity backlog.

## Status vocabulary

- Active / confirmed
- Partial / in progress
- Not in use
- Needs confirmation
- Recommended experiment

An opportunity is never displayed as active client adoption.

## Source policy

Use source tiers:

1. Official vendor documentation and release notes for product capabilities, limits, pricing, and security.
2. Independent benchmarking with published methodology for model capability, speed, and price.
3. Peer-reviewed or recognized institutional research for adoption, risk, and impact.
4. Curated discovery sources for finding candidate tools, followed by primary-source verification.

Specific approved discovery/evaluation sources:

- [FutureTools](https://futuretools.io/) for discovery and category scanning; popularity is not proof of suitability.
- [Artificial Analysis methodology](https://artificialanalysis.ai/methodology) for independently measured intelligence, performance, and cost comparisons.
- [Arena leaderboard guidance](https://help.arena.ai/articles/7011479247-how-to-see-ai-rankings-in-arena-leaderboards-2-0-wip) for human-preference rankings and confidence intervals.

No recommendation is based on one leaderboard alone. Model choice is matched to the actual task, privacy needs, integration, reliability, cost, and human review requirements.

## Refresh model

- Research snapshot carries an as-of date.
- Tool/model rankings are reviewed at least monthly while a client-facing recommendation remains active.
- Major vendor deprecations or security changes trigger an earlier review.
- Static claims expire into `Needs refresh` internally; the current release does not show generalized freshness warnings throughout the portal.
- Research updates require sanitization and owner approval before publication.

## Client-safe positioning

Recommended language:

> Third i evaluates the workflow first and the tool second. Rankings and new releases help identify candidates, but the recommendation is based on the client's work, risk, cost, and adoption path.

Avoid:

- “best AI” without a task and date;
- unsupported ROI claims;
- implying a model ranking guarantees business performance;
- exposing internal prompts;
- compliance/legal conclusions presented as professional advice;
- tool lists without implementation guidance.

## Conversion path

Every recommended experiment should have:

1. a client problem;
2. a small bounded pilot;
3. success criteria;
4. data/privacy boundaries;
5. responsible human review;
6. estimated effort and investment discussion;
7. owner/client decision point;
8. project creation link if accepted.

The accepted experiment becomes a Project, not a separate AI opportunity record.
